Introduction of Water

Water is an essential natural resource for all forms of life. It covers about 71% of the Earth's surface, mainly in oceans, rivers and lakes. Water exists in three states: solid (ice), liquid and gas (steam). It plays a vital role in maintaining ecosystems, supporting agriculture and maintaining human health. Freshwater is essential for drinking, sanitation and industry. Water also regulates the planet's temperature and weather patterns. However, pollution and scarcity pose significant global challenges, making water conservation essential. Ensuring access to clean water is crucial for sustainable development and the well-being of future generations.

One of the most popular International water research center.(IWMI)

The IWMI is a global research-for-development organization that works on improving how water and land resources are managed in developing countries. It is one of the 15 CGIAR research centers and plays a pivotal role in addressing water challenges that affect agriculture, food security, ecosystems, and livelihoods.

The importance of water in the human body.

The human body is composed of roughly 60% water, which is essential for nearly every bodily function. Water facilitates nutrient transport, temperature regulation, and waste removal. It maintains cellular integrity and chemical balance, supports metabolism, and enables efficient organ function,Water is not important. ensuring overall health and survival. Water is vital for life.

Water Composition Summary

Water (H₂O) consists of hydrogen (11.19%) and oxygen (88.81%). Natural water contains dissolved minerals like sodium, calcium, magnesium, and chloride.
